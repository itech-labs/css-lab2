About Breaking Bad

"Breaking Bad" is a critically acclaimed American television series created by Vince Gilligan. The show originally aired from 2008 to 2013 and stars Bryan Cranston as Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher turned methamphetamine manufacturing drug lord. The series is known for its intense storytelling, complex characters, and unforgettable moments.

Gallery

Walter White, portrayed by Bryan Cranston, is the central character of the series. A mild-mannered chemistry teacher who turns to cooking and selling high-quality crystal meth to secure his family's financial future after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer.

Jesse Pinkman, played by Aaron Paul, is Walter White's partner in the meth business. He's known for his colorful language and often finds himself in trouble. Jesse's character arc is one of redemption and self-discovery.

Saul Goodman, portrayed by Bob Odenkirk, is the series' morally flexible lawyer. He's known for his colorful personality and catchy slogan, "Better Call Saul." Saul is often involved in getting Walter and Jesse out of legal trouble.

Mike Ehrmantraut is a police veteran and the main security consultant for protagonist Walter White. He is noted for his professionalism and composure. Mike solves the most difficult tasks, being in the shadows and carefully planning his actions. His character is an important link in the world of drug trafficking, providing security and neutralizing threats to White and his business. Mike Ehrmantraut is a symbol of intelligence and dedication to his tasks, and he plays an important role in the development of events in the series.

Skyler White is the wife of the protagonist Walter White. She is noted for her determination and devotion to her family, but her life is significantly complicated by the actions of her husband, who becomes a drug kingpin. Her character adds depth and moral contradictions to the story of the series.
